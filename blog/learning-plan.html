My Learning Plan

What is your long-term goal and/or career pathway?

To become competent in coding, I want to experience everything in I.T from Web developmet, Game Development, Apps development anything that does coding I want to experince it

A description of your own strengths and limitations in relation to learning.

I am a very motivated person, when I wanna do something I won’t stop until it is done, I like to work hard and keep focused. Sometimes I can become a perfectionist which can be bad, sometimes I want things to be perfect when really it doesn’t need to be which can lead to not finishing the project or becoming unmotivated.

After high school I ended up working as a cellar operator making wine, working in a winery I gained alot of experince I ended up training alot of people from all over the world, I loved it but sometimes it was really hard trying to train someone who could barely speak english, but it taught me alot of patience and understanding.

What skills (non-technical - human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I would like to develop my communication skills and my confidence as it will be important when I enter the I.T industry and also my time management skills.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this program. This should include: An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, facilitators, and industry/community representatives What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you

I have written a weekly plan that I want to try and keep sticking to, studying at least 6 hours a day and a few hours at night, I make sure I take enough breaks during the day so I’m staying grounded, every morning I like to do a 15-minute meditation when I get up, to relax my mindset and to set my intentions for the day. I am fully committed to this course. I am happy to learn by myself and within a group, I like to get along with others and make sure everyone feels included, and also to help others when they need help, I like to treat this as a job and be professional in everything I do, staying in contact with students or the facilitator and sticking to deadlines. If I don’t know what I’m doing I am not afraid to ask questions or ask for help. If I feel myself feeling blocked it’s good to just take a break and meditate, release all the tension, and hopefully I can come back to the problem with a clear mindset and a better understanding.
