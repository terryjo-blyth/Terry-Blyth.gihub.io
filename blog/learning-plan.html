My Bootcamp Learning Plan

What is your long-term goal and/or career pathway?

My long-term goal is to be competent in full-stack programming and to use those skills to land a job, I don’t mind where start off at, I feel like once I have my foot inside the door of the I.T world opportunities will arise.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

I found during foundations that I was really good at helping others something I never realized before starting this course, I also found that I was good at problem-solving when I or someone ran into a problem. Also found that I am a quick learner which I think is important in the tech industry which is always involving. I think these strengths will help a lot during Bootcamp. One limitation I think I need to work on is my writing, I found it hard during foundations to write my blog post something I really want to improve on. Found it very complicated to put content together and put thoughts down to paper.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

Some of the biggest challenges I think I will face during Bootcamp will be, Speaking in large groups of people. Something I want to work on during Bootcamp. Also being a perfectionist all the time will challenge me a lot. During foundations, I found myself a lot trying to make things perfect when they were okay how they were. Sometimes I just needed to stop and move on to the next task or sprint. I think I just need to have confidence in everything I do during Bootcamp and back myself and I should be okay. I find when you start doubting yourself thats when problems start popping up. Just need to beleive and be confident.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

Theres alot I want to improve on during Bootcamp, I want to improve my self-confidence, being able to talk in front of large groups, to keep conversations going, give and receive feedback. I want to also improve on my english and writing as I was struggling to put thoughts to words on paper. I also want to work on my patience sometimes I feel like I get impatience and want things done straight away. Also my critical thinking and problem solving being able to plan and solve problems without getting impatience or being self critical things I really want to work on during Bootcamp.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

I would say I would just expect the same as foundations, my facilitator showed she cared and was always there to help which I really liked, it made me feel comfortable knowing there was someone we could go to if I ever felt stuck or blocked. I don’t really have much expectations from the Bootcamp team as I see its up to me if I really want this. I can’t put expectations fully on the team, when I have to be fully committed which I feel like I am .

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

To be honest I have high expectations in myself, I want to work hard and give Bootcamp my 100% all. I expect myself to be fully committed during the 9 weeks, I keep telling myself to be confident and to full back myself. I know bootcamp will be challenging for myself not just for the technical content but with core, its a challenge I am happy to take on and just want to give my all.

My Foundations Learning Plan

What is your long-term goal and/or career pathway?

Honestly, my long term goal is just to get good at what I do and get a good job. Coming from a small place like Gisborne, there's not many opportunities here and I want to experience much more and I feel like the I.T industry can help with that.

A description of your own strengths and limitations in relation to learning.

I am a very motivated person, when I wanna do something I won’t stop until it is done, I like to work hard and keep focused. Sometimes I can doubt myself and think I can’t do it, I just need to stay positive and believe in myself.

What skills (non-technical - human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

My social skills to learn to keep conversations going, talking in large groups. My confidence in being able to believe in myself.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this program

I have written a weekly plan that I want to try and keep to, studying at least 6-8 hours a day and a few hours at night, I make sure I take enough breaks during the day so I’m staying grounded, every morning I like to do a 15-minute meditation when I get up, to relax my mind and to set my intentions for the day. I am fully committed to this course. I am happy to learn by myself and within a group, I like to get along with others and make sure everyone feels included, and also to help others when they need help, I like to treat this as a job and be professional in everything I do, staying in contact with students or the facilitator and sticking to deadlines. If I don’t know what I’m doing I am not afraid to ask questions or ask for help. If I feel myself feeling blocked it’s good to just take a break and meditate, release all the tension.
